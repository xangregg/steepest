Steepest roads

Road data © OpenStreetMap contributors via Overpass API. Elevation from AWS Terrain Tiles (≈10–30 m resolution — short-segment grades are approximate; bridge and tunnel elevations are interpolated between their ends).

How to read this map

  • Color is steepness. Roads are tinted on a 5–25 % grade scale: red for top-ranked items, purple for other steep segments.
  • The amber underlay marks long inclines — mostly-monotonic stretches averaging ≥ 2.25 %. Inclines can span several connected roads.
  • Pick what to rank by:
    Steepest
    best average grade over the sustained length
    Hardest climb
    gain × grade of a continuous climb
    Longest incline
    length, possibly across multiple roads
  • The list on the right ranks the top roads. Hover a row to highlight it on the map; click to zoom in. The CSV button downloads the list data.
  • Click any road segment for its detailed grade and elevation data.

Elevations come from ~10–30 m DEM data, so short-segment grades are approximate — longer sustained lengths resist that noise.
